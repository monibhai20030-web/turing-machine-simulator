The Efficiency Advantage ($O(n)$):
This multi-tape machine solves the palindrome problem much faster than a standard single-tape machine.
  1. COPY: Tape 1 reads the input. Tape 2 concurrently writes a copy, moving its head right.
  2. REWIND: The machine rewinds Tape 2's head back to the beginning.
  3. CHECK: Both tapes read simultaneously. Tape 1 reads Left-to-Right, while Tape 2 reads Right-to-Left (relative to original input).
While a single-tape machine requires $O(n^2)$ steps (shuttling back and forth for every pair), this machine finishes in a small multiple of $n$ steps.
